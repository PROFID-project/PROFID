Overview

The ambition of PROFID is to reassess the role of routine prophylactic ICD implantation in patients with reduced LVEF ≤ 35% after myocardial infarction under contemporary OMT and fundamentally improve clinical practice, potentially reducing the enormous burden of sudden cardiac death on society and patients.

PROFID is expected to change SCD prevention in clinical practice. To achieve this, the project is structured into two main phases: Data Analysis and a Clinical Trial.

Data Analysis

In the first phase, a variety of data from different sources will be analysed. This includes highly phenotyped data from ~200,000 post-myocardial infarction patients of both sexes, drawn from national registries, research databases, electronic health records, and claims databases. The repository contains code for cohort studies within the PROFID data lake, to be submitted for publication in 2026.

Clinical Trial

In the second phase, a non-inferiority randomised clinical trial will compare OMT versus ICD implantation plus OMT in post-MI patients with LVEF ≤ 35% who would currently receive an ICD according to guidelines. The ultimate goal is to prevent the majority of catastrophic sudden cardiac death events after myocardial infarction.

Installation & Pipelines

Instructions for installing and running the preprocessing pipelines are included in the preprocessing folder. These detail imputation and sample size calculation processes from the initial PROFID study analysis.

Usage examples are provided with the scripts.
