Prevention of Sudden Cardiac Death after Myocardial Infarction

Reassessing prophylactic ICD implantation under contemporary optimal medical therapy

About PROFID

The ambition of PROFID is to reassess the role of routine prophylactic ICD implantation in patients with reduced LVEF ≤ 35% after myocardial infarction under contemporary optimal medical therapy (OMT).

By fundamentally improving clinical practice, PROFID aims to substantially reduce the enormous burden that sudden cardiac death places on both society and individual patients.

Project Phases

Data Analysis

PROFID analyses highly phenotyped data from approximately 200,000 post-myocardial infarction patients across national registries, institutional databases, EHRs and claims data.

This repository contains analytical pipelines used to conduct cohort studies within the PROFID data lake, planned for publication in 2026.

Clinical Trial

A non-inferiority randomised clinical trial comparing optimal medical therapy alone versus ICD implantation plus OMT in post-MI patients with LVEF ≤ 35%.

The goal is to optimise patient selection for ICD therapy while maintaining safety.
